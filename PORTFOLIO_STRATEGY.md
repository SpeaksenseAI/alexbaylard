# Broadway & Musical Theatre Portfolio Strategy
**Prepared for Alex Baylard**

To capture the attention of Broadway casting directors, talent agents, and creatives, an actor's website must be ruthlessly efficient, highly visual, and optimized for quick decision-making. Casting directors often make initial judgments within seconds. Your website is your digital audition room.

This document outlines the strategic approach and structural requirements for your new portfolio website.

---

## 1. Core Philosophy: Signal Over Noise
Casting directors are busy. They don't want to hunt for your materials. The goal is to provide **frictionless access** to your primary casting materials:
1. Headshots (High-resolution, instantly visible)
2. Reels (Sizzle, Vocal, Dance—embedded, no downloading required)
3. Resume (Clear web version + 1-click PDF download)
4. Contact Info (Agent/Manager details prominently displayed)

## 2. Site Architecture & Skeleton

### The Hero Section (First Impression)
- **Visuals:** A stunning, high-res cinematic photo or a silent background video montage of your best stage work. 
- **Copy:** "Alex Baylard" — prominent and clear.
- **Sub-headline:** "Actor • Singer • Dancer" (or your specific union status: AEA / SAG-AFTRA).
- **Call to Action (CTA):** "Watch Reel" and "Download Resume". These are the two most important actions a casting director will take.

### Media & Reels (The "Show Me" Section)
- **Layout:** Embedded video players (YouTube/Vimeo) so they play instantly on the site.
- **Categorization:**
  - *Sizzle Reel* (2-3 minutes max, showing emotional range and versatility).
  - *Vocal Reel* (Distinct cuts: Contemporary Musical Theatre, Golden Age, Pop/Rock).
  - *Dance Reel* (If applicable, showing technical proficiency and styles).
- **Strategy:** Never make them click away to another site or download a massive video file.

### Headshots & Gallery
- **Primary Headshots:** 2-4 pristine, professional headshots (Theatrical, Commercial, Legit).
- **Production Stills:** A curated masonry grid of high-quality production photos. Show, don't just tell, that you are a working actor.
- **Functionality:** Lightbox capability so photos can be clicked and expanded, with an option to download high-res files for press or casting.

### Resume
- **Web Version:** A clean, easy-to-read HTML table of your credits categorized by:
  - Broadway / National Tours
  - Regional Theatre
  - Film / Television
  - Training / Education
  - Special Skills (Crucial for unique casting needs: acrobatics, instruments, dialects).
- **Download:** A highly visible "Download Resume (PDF)" button.

### News / Updates
- A brief section highlighting current or upcoming projects. E.g., "Currently playing [Role] in [Show] at [Theatre]."
- Shows momentum and that you are an active, hireable professional.

### Contact & Representation
- Your primary agency/management contact details (Email, Phone, Name of Agent).
- Direct contact form (optional, often better to just list an email or use a mailto link).
- Links to professional profiles: Actors Access, Casting Networks, Backstage, IMDb.
- Link to Instagram (if it is curated professionally).

---

## 3. Design & UX Strategy

- **Mobile-First:** Casting directors and agents will often look you up on their phones while in the room or on the go. The site must be flawlessly responsive.
- **Navigation:** Sticky header with clear anchor links (`#reels`, `#resume`, `#headshots`).
- **Typography:** Clean, legible sans-serif or modern serif fonts. Avoid overly stylized or script fonts that are hard to read.
- **Dark/Light Mode:** A modern touch that looks premium and accommodates user preferences.

## 4. Assets Required from Alex (To fill the skeleton)

1. **Videos:** YouTube or Vimeo links for 1-3 reels (Acting, Singing, Dancing).
2. **Images:** 
   - 1-2 massive, high-res landscape images for backgrounds/hero.
   - 2-4 professional headshots (portrait).
   - 6-12 production stills from past shows.
3. **Resume:** A current PDF of the 1-page acting resume.
4. **Copy:** 
   - A short, 3-4 sentence professional bio.
   - Current news/upcoming projects.
   - Agent/Manager contact information.
   - Union status (AEA, EMC, SAG-AFTRA, etc.).
   - Height, Vocal Range.
